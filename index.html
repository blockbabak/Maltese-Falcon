The Maltese Falcon: A True Health Mystery

“The stuff that dreams are made of.”
— The Maltese Falcon, 1941

In the film, the legendary Falcon turns out to be a fraud — a glittering decoy fought over by men who didn’t care who got hurt, as long as it wasn’t them.

Sadly, this isn’t fiction.

I’m Babak Kamali — British citizen, semi-retired engineer, former CTO, diagnosed with ADHD and aortic valve disease long before arriving in Malta in 2015. What followed was a near-decade of medical negligence, indifference, and systemic evasion. Promises were made. Treatments delayed. Lives (mine) quietly placed at risk.

It started with a malfunctioning medical implant. Then a worsening heart condition. What I got in return was not care — but avoidance. Bureaucrats vanished, doctors contradicted themselves, records went silent. And when challenged, the system seemed more concerned with cutting costs than saving lives.

I now believe this is not just incompetence — but targeted victimization of a foreigner who dared to demand continuity of care. Had I been Maltese, I suspect this saga would’ve played out differently.

What This Site Contains

  • 💔 Heart-Story: My journey through cardiac neglect, contradictory diagnoses, and a quietly ticking clock.
  • 🧠 Brain-Story: The long silence around a medical device that should’ve been replaced or reactivated years ago — but wasn’t.

All claims are supported by emails, diagnostics, reports, and message logs — available on request. These stories are just the surface. The full evidence runs deeper.

Because I’m running out of time — and options.
